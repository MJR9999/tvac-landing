What TVAC does

A serious evaluation engine for tokenization – not just another chatbot.

TVAC takes a structured description of your asset, jurisdiction, structure and investor target group and converts it into a consistent, transparent value-add assessment – including a go / borderline / no-go verdict.

What TVAC gives you

For each project, TVAC returns:

  • A verdict: go / borderline / no-go.
  • Scores for New Opportunities, Cost Savings, Risk Reduction, Tokenization Costs and New Risks.
  • A consolidated value-add score.
  • A written explanation of why the project scores the way it does.
  • Early-stage suggestions on how to improve the design and value-add profile.
Framework-driven JSON-structured output Case-specific reasoning

How it works in three steps

  1. You describe your case. Asset type, jurisdiction, investor target group, structure, goals and constraints – plus a short free-text explanation.
  2. TVAC evaluates the design. An AI model, guided by a strict instruction set, scores the project against the five value-add factors and applies the core formula.
  3. You get a structured verdict. A clear go / borderline / no-go outcome, factor scores, and a narrative explanation you can use internally or with clients.

Under the hood, TVAC is a React frontend and a Node/Express API running on Render, with the evaluation powered by an OpenAI model. You interact with a product – not a raw model.

Why TVAC?

Why pay for TVAC if it “just calls OpenAI”?

Because you are not paying for raw API access. You are paying for a domain-specific, consistent evaluation machine that turns messy tokenization ideas into comparable, structured verdicts.

A fixed framework instead of prompt roulette

Every TVAC run uses the same strict methodology:

  • A defined value-add formula and five-factor structure.
  • Clear rules for how to derive a verdict.
  • Input completeness and confidence checks.

Opening ChatGPT in a browser means reinventing that structure from scratch each time – with no guarantee of consistency.

Productized expertise, not just a model

TVAC bakes in a lot of hard-won thinking about when tokenization actually adds value – and when it does not. It also guarantees:

  • Stable, machine-readable JSON output.
  • A dedicated UI for non-technical users.
  • A path to benchmarking, exports and integration into your workflows.

You could approximate this by hand with ChatGPT – but it would be manual, fragile and impossible to scale or compare over time.

FAQ

A few common questions

Is this legal, financial or investment advice?

No. TVAC is a decision-support tool, not a licensed adviser. It provides structured evaluations and explanations based on your input and a defined framework. It is meant to inform and sharpen internal discussions – not to replace professional advice.

Does TVAC use live market data?

Not in the current version. TVAC combines (1) what you tell it about your case, and (2) the model’s general domain knowledge about tokenization, regulation, costs and risk patterns. It does not connect to live pricing feeds, trading venues or on-chain analytics yet.

Will I get the same result every time?

TVAC aims for high consistency: similar cases should produce similar verdicts and score patterns. Small variations in wording may change details of the explanation, but the overall profile should remain stable for the same design.

Can we get a customized version?

In principle, yes. TVAC can be tuned to specific jurisdictions, asset types or internal policies, and integrated via API into your existing tools. The current early-access phase is the right moment to explore such collaborations.
